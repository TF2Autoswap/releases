# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as contributors and maintainers of TF2autoswap pledge to make participation
in this project and its community a harassment-free experience for everyone,
regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics,
gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic
status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity
and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming,
and healthy community.

---

## Our Standards

Examples of behaviour that contributes to a positive environment:

- Being respectful of differing viewpoints, experience levels, and playstyles
- Giving and accepting constructive feedback graciously
- Taking responsibility when mistakes are made and learning from them
- Focusing on what is best for the community as a whole
- Showing empathy towards other community members

Examples of unacceptable behaviour:

- Harassment, insults, or derogatory comments of any kind
- Trolling, deliberately derailing conversations, or sustained disruption
- Publishing private information about others without explicit permission
- Personal or political attacks unrelated to the project
- Any conduct that would reasonably be considered inappropriate in a
  collaborative setting

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all project spaces — the GitHub repository,
issue tracker, wiki, pull requests, and any other official communication
channels. It also applies when an individual is representing the project
publicly.

---

## Enforcement

Instances of unacceptable behaviour may be reported by opening a GitHub issue
marked **[CoC]**, or by contacting the maintainer directly via GitHub.

All reports will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly. The
maintainer is obligated to maintain confidentiality with regard to the reporter.

Depending on severity, responses may include: a warning, temporary restriction
from project spaces, or a permanent ban.
